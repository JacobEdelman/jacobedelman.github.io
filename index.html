HSCTF

I am the President and Founder of HSCTF, an international week-long cybersecurity and programming competition educating over 2,300 high school students from 250 U.S. high schools and 40+ countries. In a CTF, teams compete online to solve increasingly difficult challenges for points (learning as they progress). HSCTF was the first CTF (and contiues to be the largest) designed and run by high school students. HSCTF was the first to extend the CTF model beyond computer secrurity (cryptography, reverse engineering, forensics, and recon) to include other areas of computer science such as the design and analysis of algorithms and programming languages. HSCTF 1 and 2 was sponsored by Facebook, among others. HSCTF 3 will take place May 2016.

HSCS.io

I am the President and Founder of HSCS.io, an incubator to help high schools launch their own cybersecurity competitions. HSCS.io has helped 6 CTFs launch to-date with over 500 high schools participating in these competitions.
